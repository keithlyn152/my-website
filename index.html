About Educare College, Inc.

Educare College, Inc. (ECI) is a private school that offers quality and affordable education. Since it was founded, the school has been dedicated to helping students become responsible, skilled, and ready to compete in the modern world. It focuses on academics, building good character, and encouraging students to take part in the community. In today’s digital age, education should be easy to access and connected. That’s why our official website is here, it serves as the school’s online identity and a central place where students, parents, teachers, and future enrollees can find important information. From academic programs and admission steps to announcements, events, and achievements, this site is designed to keep everyone updated and involved. At Educare College, Inc., we want learning to be meaningful and communication to be simple. By strengthening our online presence, we continue to fulfill our mission of giving quality education and preparing students for success in a fast-changing world.

Tracks Offered

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM)

The Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) strand is designed for students who are passionate about science, innovation, mathematics, and technology. This academic track equips learners with critical thinking, analytical, and problem-solving skills through hands-on activities, laboratory experiments, and research-based learning.

Students in the STEM strand study subjects such as advanced mathematics, biology, chemistry, physics, computer science, and engineering concepts. The curriculum prepares students for college programs related to medicine, engineering, architecture, information technology, computer science, environmental science, and other STEM-related fields.

Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM)

The Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM) strand is designed for students who are interested in business, finance, entrepreneurship, and management. It equips learners with the knowledge and skills needed to understand business operations, financial management, marketing, and organizational leadership.

Students in the ABM strand study subjects such as accounting, business mathematics, economics, marketing, business ethics, and entrepreneurship. These subjects prepare students to pursue business-related courses in college and develop the confidence to manage or establish their own businesses in the future.

Home Economics (HE)

The Home Economics (HE) strand is designed for students who are interested in hospitality, culinary arts, food and beverage services, housekeeping, tourism, fashion, and other livelihood-related fields. It provides learners with practical knowledge and hands-on skills that prepare them for employment, entrepreneurship, or higher education.

Students in the HE strand study subjects related to food preparation, baking and pastry production, housekeeping, caregiving, dressmaking, beauty care, and business management. Through practical training and real-world experiences, students develop the skills necessary to provide quality products and services in various industries.

Information and Communications Technology (ICT)

The Information and Communications Technology (ICT) strand is designed for students who are interested in computers, programming, networking, and digital technologies. It equips learners with the knowledge and practical skills needed to use, develop, and manage computer systems and information technology solutions in today's digital world.

Students in the ICT strand study subjects such as computer programming, web development, computer hardware servicing, networking, database management, and multimedia design. Through hands-on activities and project-based learning, students develop technical skills that prepare them for college, employment, or entrepreneurship in the field of information technology.

Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS)

The Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) strand is designed for students who are interested in understanding human behavior, society, culture, communication, and public service. It develops learners' critical thinking, communication, leadership, and interpersonal skills through the study of social sciences, literature, philosophy, and the arts.

Students in the HUMSS strand study subjects such as psychology, sociology, political science, communication, creative writing, and world religions. These subjects prepare students to analyze social issues, communicate effectively, and become responsible citizens who can contribute positively to their communities.
